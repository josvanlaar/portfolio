"Scuttling the u-529" (2025)

Unreal Engine 5.5

"Scuttling the U-529" is another homage to one of my all-time favorite video games: Medal of Honor: Allied Assault (2002).

With the power and fidelity of Unreal Engine 5.5, I was able to recreate and remaster one of the most iconic missions: the undercover mission inside the German submarine pen in Norway. This time I opted for an ingame walkthrough video,
as if it's an actual playable experience. It was well received with many game pages writing about it.

My work on "Scuttling the U-529" included:

  • Rebuilding the base geometry, using Unreal Engine 5's Modeling Tools
  • Asset integration and material creation
  • Full prop- and set dressing
  • Full lighting pass using Lumen
  • Character (Metahuman) integration, rigging and animating it using Control Rig
  • Created proprietary tools to streamline development
  • Cinematic implementation using Unreal Engine's Level Sequencer
    • Character (NPC) behaviors

Medal of Honor: Allied Assault (2002) is a videogame with a special place my heart. Spending most of my teenage years with this game, I loved every minute of it.

After the success of my first Medal of Honor Reimagined video,
I was curious what it would take to remake one of MoHAA's iconic missions: the undercover mission inside the German submarine pens. I remember this tense mission due to its undercover nature, and swigching up from the intense action the game otherwise portrays.
